Journalism (at The i)

Politics

Universal Credit and the impact on 'red wall' seats (Project, Code) R [Oct 2021] [The i]

I used data from the Department of Work and Pensions to uncover the Conservative constituencies which would be most affected by the cut to Universal Credit. I then interviewed food bank workers and experts on Universal Credit to get a better idea of the potential effect of the cuts. Liaising with the politics team, I was also able to incorporate the responses of some of the Conservative MPs who represented these constituencies.

How Le Pen won in the majority Muslim island of Mayotte (Project) R [April 2022] [The i]

"While analysing the commune-level returns from the first round of the French presidential election I discovered that Marine Le Pen's biggest win was in the overseas department of Mayotte, an island which is 98 per cent Muslim. I then spoke to people in Mayotte to discover why Le Pen had become so popular in the island despite her Islamophobic history.

Facebook Ads (Project, Code) Python, R [June 2021] [The i]

As part of The i's coverage of the Batley and Spen by-election, I used my access to the Facebook Ad Library to evaluate how many adverts the Conservative and Labour parties were placing in Batley and Spen ahead of the by-election. I then compared this to the level of advertising in the two previous by-elections.

Investigations

Channel migrants (Project 1, Project 2) R [December 2021] [The i] - Nominated for DRUM award, Best Investigation

I used GIS ship tracking data from MarineTraffic during the course of the search for the migrant boat in order to identify key details about the search for the missing migrants. I used the R SF package to convert the raw GIS data into SHP files. From this, Dean and I were able to work out the methods used for searching for the migrants as well as the key players involved with the investigation.

Russian misinformation (Project) Python, R, Gephi [March 2022] [The i]

I used the Twitter API along with Selenium to identify Russian embassy accounts that were spreading misinformation, despite the same tweets being deleted from other accounts. I then used Selenium to identify a large number of embassy accounts that were not labelled as government-affiliated organisations, in violation of Twitter’s own policy on labelling accounts.

TikTok videos spreading life-threatening DIY abortion advice (Project) NodeJS Python [July 2022] [The i]

I used the NodeJS TikTok scraper, combined with custom webhooks and proxies, to analyse over 1,000 TikTok videos in the wake of RoevWade and found that a considerable number were offering life endangering abortion advice. TikTok also rarely provided warnings on these videos.

Economics

How IR35 tax changes fuelled the lorry driver crisis (Project) [October 2021] [The i]

I broke the story explaining how changes to the rules that determine whether a worker is self-employed led to a massive exodus of truck drivers from the UK. The story gained significant traction on Twitter and was commented on by people such as Robert Colville, director of the Centre for Policy Studies. It was uncovered by speaking to a huge number of truck drivers about their issues at work.

The story behind the petrol shortage (Project) [October 2021] [The i]

Using data on fuel supply at forecourts from BEIS I explained how the fuel shortage was more akin to a conventional bank run, rather than an actual long term shortage. The article was retweeted almost a thousand times and was commented on and shared by multiple Conservative MPs.

Covid-19

Record number of NHS staff quit (Project) R [January 2021] [The i] - Front-page story (exclusive)

I broke the story that a record number of NHS staff had quit during the pandemic, despite many staff staying on during the first few months of the pandemic. I used R to analyse the data and spoke to multiple NHS staff members about the difficulties they faced during the pandemic and why some had chosen to quit.

Immensa Labs (Project) R [October 2021] [The i]

I used exclusive UKHSA data provided to me by an academic to detect which areas had been worst affected by the Immensa testing lab scandal. From there I used routine Covid-19 data to demonstrate how the most affected local authorities had seen a massive rise in Covid-19 cases in the wake of the scandal which was not replicated elsewhere in the country.

Continuous Trackers / Models

Politics

UK Poll Tracker, (Project, Code) and Opinion Poll Tracker(Project, Code) Scraping: Python (Selenium). Model: R (brms package). Front-end: JavaScript (d3), HTML, CSS. [July-Sept 2021/Feb 2020] [The i]

I collaborated with researchers from Manchester University to build the most advanced poll tracker in use by a UK national newspaper. The tracker uses a Bayesian Dirichlet model to estimate the latent voter intention, with confidence intervals, for all major parties up until the latest poll. The model dynamically adjusts for sampling size, the date of the poll and the specific pollster house.

To compliment the voting tracker I built a separate tracker than shows the popularity of Boris Johnson and Kier Starmer over time. Tracker is built in a similar fashion to the original one with a simple LOESS model to estimate latent popularity.

2020 US Election Model, (Project, Code). Back-end: R, STAN. Front-end: d3.js, JavaScript, HTML, CSS. [November 2020] [Other]

During the first lockdown of the pandemic, I built a state-space model to predict the result of the 2020 election, designed as a STAN implementation of Drew Linzer's 2008 paper on the subject, with correlated priors. The model correctly predicted one less state than The Economist's + 538. I wrote a full breakdown of every model's performance here.

The i Local Elections(Project). QGIS, R , Node, d3, Mapbox, Datawrapper, GitHub Actions [February 2020] [The i]

I built multiple trackers for the 2022 local elections, starting with a custom map built in QGIS which contained every geography up for election, including NI assembly constituencies and counties yet to appear on other maps. I then constructed a map using Mapbox to visualise the results, along with a postcode detector and topline counter. The results were fed through a google spreadsheet connected to a GitHub Action, allowing multiple people to input results at once.

Tory Leadership Odds Tracker (Project) Svelte d3 Python (Selenium) [July 2022] [The i]

I built a political odds tracker, which collected and analysed the betting odds from 25 different companies over 200 different candidates to work out who was considered the most likely to win the the Conservative leadership contest.

Tory Leadership Endorsement Tracker (Project) Svelte d3 Python (Selenium/Pandas) [July 2022] [The i]

I wrote a program to track which MPs had endorsed each leadership candidate. The program then used the House of Commons Members API to compute each MPs level of experience, region, gender and intake year.

Interactive projects

Politics

Trump's Tweets, (Project, Code) d3js, JavaScript, HTML, CSS [January 2021] [Other]

A graphic display of all of Trump's tweets since his 2016 candidacy announcement to his Twitter suspension, accounting for favourites, retweets, and whether the tweet was later deleted or flagged. The visualisation features a tooltip that dynamically wrangles the base data using RegEx so that it can be rendered in the style of a typical tweet.

Economics

What Happened to the US Treasuries in March? (Project, Code) d3js, jQuery, CSS, HTML [November 2020] [Other]

A scrolling narrative to explain in an elegant manner what happened to the US treasury market during the Covid-19 crisis. The scrollytelling framework which underpins this story does not rely on 3rd party libraries but was designed by hand for this project. It was built using a mixture of CSS, jQuery and d3.

Inflation Calculator (Project) R Svelte d3 [July 2022] [The i]

Using work from the IFS, Resolution Foundation and ONS I built an Inflation calculator to determine your personal inflation rate using information such as how much you earn, whether you own a car and how much you spend on rent.

Other Projects

A Study of Twitter (Project, Code) Fasttext (for NLP), Gephi (Network analysis), Front end: CSS, HTML, jQuery, Plotly [April 2020] [Other]

I designed this project as a way of charting ‘British political twitter’ while also investigating which voices have the most influence on social media. I used network analysis through Gephi, along with the Twitter API, to uncover the particular communities within political Twitter. I also used Facebook’s Machine Learning library, Fasttext, to analyse the sentiment of each tweet, which I used to chart the divisiveness of particular topics.

Chexit.co.uk (Project, Code) PHP + MySQL, Ajax, JavaScript, CSS + HTML [September 2019] [Other]

During the Brexit saga, I designed this database of every MP’s vote on over 50 different Brexit divisions. It was intended as a resource through which anyone could find an easily digestible summary of an MP’s Brexit position.
