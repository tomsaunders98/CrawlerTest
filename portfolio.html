Journalism (at The i)

Politics

Universal Credit and the impact on 'red wall' seats (Project, Code) R [Oct 2021] [The i]

I used data from the Department of Work and Pensions to uncover the Conservative constituencies which would be most affected by the cut to Universal Credit. I then interviewed food bank workers and experts on Universal Credit to get a better idea of the potential effect of the cuts. Liaising with the politics team, I was also able to incorporate the responses of some of the Conservative MPs who represented these constituencies.

New Constituencies Map (Project) R (sf) [June 2021] [The i]

For The i's politics team, I modelled the political composition of the new proposed constituencies. I did this by regressing the ward-level results from the 2021 local elections to the new constituencies through geospatial analysis and the simple features package for R. I picked up on new potential gains for Greens, something which was later corroborated by Ben Walker in the New Statesman.

Facebook Ads (Project, Code) Python, R [June 2021] [The i]

As part of The i's coverage of the Batley and Spen by-election, I used my access to the Facebook Ad Library to evaluate how many adverts the Conservative and Labour parties were placing in Batley and Spen ahead of the by-election. I then compared this to the level of advertising in the two previous by-elections.

Economics

How IR35 tax changes fuelled the lorry driver crisis (Project) [October 2021] [The i]

I broke the story explaining how changes to the rules that determine whether a worker is self-employed led to a massive exodus of truck drivers from the UK. The story gained significant traction on Twitter and was commented on by people such as Robert Colville, director of the Centre for Policy Studies. The story has almost 70,000 views to date and was among The i's most popular articles. It was uncovered by speaking to a huge number of truck drivers about their issues at work. From this I also wrote another article on the problems that truck drivers face, which was read by over 20,000 people.

The story behind the petrol shortage (Project) [October 2021] [The i]

Using data on fuel supply at forecourts from BEIS I explained how the fuel shortage was more akin to a conventional bank run, rather than actual long term shortage. The article was retweeted almost a thousand times and was commented on and shared by multiple Conservative MPs.

'3 million homes on the Greenbelt' (Project, Code), QGIS [September 2021] [The i]

I used data from Ordinance Survey on train station locations, as well as spatial data from MHCLG on the greenbelt and other specially designated areas, to calculate how much of the greenbelt was within a 10-minute walk (1 km radius) of a train station but did not contain any other special designation. I then employed simple housing density calculations to work out that this land could theoretically provide over 3 million homes. I also spoke to MPs and greenbelt groups to build a picture of what unused green belt land looks like in the UK.

'The inequalities of Council Tax' (Project, Code), R [September 2021] [The i]

I used data on council tax rates to work out which areas of the UK had the highest comparative burden of council tax compared to the average income and house price. I uncovered that in the UK's wealthiest areas council tax can represents just 0.1% of the average property price with this rising to 2% in the UK's poorest areas. I interviewed members of the Fairer Share campaign group, along with academics such as John Muellbauer, to illustrate the inequality in clear detail.

Covid-19

Why are Covid-19 rates falling (Project, Code) R, Modelr for rolling projections [August 2021] [The i]

When Covid-19 cases began to inexplicably fall, despite projections they would rise beyond 100k a day, I examined all the different hypothesises for the fall in cases. I used Modelr to build rolling linear projections to investigate vaccine levels and I employed warming stripes, pioneered by climatologist Ed Hawkins, to investigate whether the recent temperature rise was correlated to the fall in cases.

Scotland Covid-19 cases (Project, Code) R [September 2021] [The i]

In anticipation of the reopening of schools in England, I looked at data from Public Health Scotland, as schools had reopened there weeks earlier, to better understand the effect of school reopening on Covid-19 cases. I interviewed epidemiologists from the University of Edinburgh and discovered that even with a high vaccine uptake for those aged 16-17 there was still a large rise in Covid-19 cases compared to when schools last reopened.

Continuous Trackers / Models

Politics

The i UK Poll Tracker, (Project, Code). Scraping: Python (Selenium). Model: R (brms package). Front-end: JavaScript (d3), HTML, CSS. [July-Sept 2021] [The i]

I collaborated with researchers from Manchester University to build the most advanced poll tracker in use by a UK national newspaper. The tracker uses a Bayesian Dirichlet model to estimate the latent voter intention, with confidence intervals, for all major parties up until the latest poll. The model dynamically adjusts for sampling size, the date of the poll and the specific pollster house.

2020 US Election Model, (Project, Code). Back-end: R, STAN. Front-end: d3.js, JavaScript, HTML, CSS. [November 2020] [Other]

During the first lockdown of the pandemic, I built a state-space model to predict the result of the 2020 election, designed as a STAN implementation of Drew Linzer's 2008 paper on the subject, with correlated priors. The model correctly predicted one less state than The Economist's + 538. I wrote a full breakdown of every model's performance here.

Covid-19

The i Covid-19 Tracker. (Project, Code). Python, R [June 2021] [The i]

I built a Python wrapper for the Covid-19 dashboard API v2 (documentation only existed for v1). I used this with data from Our World in Data to supply live interactive Datawrapper charts that are used across The i (350,000 views in the last month). The data also supplies my Covid-19 tracker visuals, which are built in R and generated daily. R was chosen instead of d3js as there was no way at the time of integrating d3 into The i's CMS.

Interactive projects

Politics

Trump's Tweets, (Project, Code) d3js, JavaScript, HTML, CSS [January 2021] [Other]

A graphic display of all of Trump's tweets since his 2016 candidacy announcement to his Twitter suspension, accounting for favourites, retweets, and whether the tweet was later deleted or flagged. The visualisation features a tooltip that dynamically wrangles the base data using RegEx so that it can be rendered in the style of a typical tweet.

Economics

What Happened to the US Treasuries in March? (Project, Code) d3js, jQuery, CSS, HTML [November 2020] [Other]

A scrolling narrative to explain in an elegant manner what happened to the US treasury market during the Covid-19 crisis. The scrollytelling framework which underpins this story does not rely on 3rd party libraries but was designed by hand for this project. It was built using a mixture of CSS, jQuery and d3.

Other Projects

A Study of Twitter (Project, Code) Fasttext (for NLP), Gephi (Network analysis), Front end: CSS, HTML, jQuery, Plotly [April 2020] [Other]

I designed this project as a way of charting ‘British political twitter’ while also investigating which voices have the most influence on social media. I used network analysis through Gephi, along with the Twitter API, to uncover the particular communities within political Twitter. I also used Facebook’s Machine Learning library, Fasttext, to analyse the sentiment of each tweet, which I used to chart the divisiveness of particular topics.

Chexit.co.uk (Project, Code) PHP + MySQL, Ajax, JavaScript, CSS + HTML [September 2019] [Other]

During the Brexit saga, I designed this database of every MP’s vote on over 50 different Brexit divisions. It was intended as a resource through which anyone could find an easily digestible summary of an MP’s Brexit position.
