Ex Durham University Head of Department suspended from new role following allegations of failure to pay rent

By Charlotte Evans on November 3, 2019

A former Durham University head of department within the Business School has been suspended from his current role of Principal and Vice Chancellor at the University of Dundee. He had taken up the position, which is estimated to pay around Â£300,000 per year on January 1st 2019, with the suspension arising just nine months later on September 13th.

It is alleged that Professor Andrew Atherton failed to pay rent for accommodation which was being provided to him through the employer.

The Courier has alleged that Professor Atherton was staying at University House, on Perth Road, when he failed to cover the full cost of rent.

During his time at Durham University from 1995 to 2002, the Professor worked as a director of the Policy Research Unit within the Small Business Centre, before becoming Head of Department.

A Mandarin Chinese speaker, he has also advised both national and regional government as well as various agencies on enterprise and small business policy development and implementation.

In addition to holding positions at Durham and Dundee universities, Professor Atherton also worked for the University of Lincoln as the Senior Deputy Vice-Chancellor and played an important role in the creation of the new School of Engineering, the first of its kind in the UK for 20 years. It was created alongside Siemens and opened for use in 2011.

He also held the role of Deputy Vice- Chancellor and Professor of Enterprise at Lancaster University, and is said to be an expert in China and SME policy.
