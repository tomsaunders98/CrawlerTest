A Quick Guide to Northern Ireland’s Political Parties

By deputy.editor on November 6, 2019

Amidst the chaos of Brexit, Britain has suddenly remembered about the other piece of its Kingdom.

 But what of the politics of this strange land of bonfires and borders? Ahead of the upcoming election, The Monocle makes a brief inquiry into Northern Ireland’s political parties.

The Main Contenders

Democratic Unionist Party: We kick things off with the good folks who brought you homophobia and climate change denial. Still up in arms about the introduction of equal marriage and reproductive rights, expect them to make the most of their final gasp of relevance on a UK-wide level. Quite impressive in that each of their MPs manages to be individually dislikeable.

Sinn Féin: The abstentionist republicans have made a strong start to their campaign to out-twat the DUP by having IRA bomber and convicted mass murderer Sean Kelly canvass for one of their candidates. A further slip of the mask of inclusiveness came when their Queen’s University society displayed a ‘Brits Out’ banner during their Fresher’s Fair. But you can’t be wrong if you’re always the victim, right?

Ulster Unionist Party: The more moderate unionist party’s incoming party leader Steve Aiken vowed to stand up to the DUP, and then immediately backtracked on a promise to field a candidate in every constituency to avoid splitting the unionist vote in North Belfast. That’ll show ‘em you’re not just a doormat.

Social Democratic Labour Party: Sinn Féin’s more vanilla cousins are seeking to claw back moderate nationalist votes. As with the UUP, they’ve been steadily losing votes to the hardliners in recent years. You’d almost feel sorry for them if they weren’t so incredibly dull.

Alliance Party: This liberal, non-sectarian party has long been the subject of scorn for its attempts to see past the religious divide. How dare they try to build a better future? The political equivalent of a plain ham sandwich and ready salted crisps meal deal.

And the Rest

Traditional Unionist Voice: Yes, this pro-Union party manages to make the DUP look a bit tame. Essentially the one-man-band of ex-DUP grandee Jim Allister, it remains to be seen whether they’ll bother standing. 

Green Party:They’re basically a Prius-driving Alliance Party but with an even more self-congratulatory support base. You can be sure all five of their voters will be sobbing over their quinoa when they don’t win any seats.

Aontú: This new all-Ireland party is the brainchild of republicans impressed by the DUP’s example of turning the Old Testament into a political manifesto. The name means (*checks notes*) ‘unity’, which seems apt considering how they’ve managed to unite all the bad points of the DUP and Sinn Féin into one political abomination.

People Before Profit: As the name suggests, they’re not big on the whole capitalism thing. It must be hard to start a revolution when nobody knows who you are.

NI Conservatives: So irrelevant that Boris Johnson probably doesn’t know they exist. Essentially the San Marino football team of the political scene, you have them some credit for persisting even whilst getting decimated in every election.
