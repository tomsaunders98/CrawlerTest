Hundreds Gather for BLM Protest in Durham

By Tom Saunders on June 6, 2020

Hundreds of students and Durhm locals turned out for the Black Lives Matter protest in Durham today. The peaceful protest began at Palace Green where protesters took a knee in solidarity with the Black Lives Matter movement.

Around 450 protests met in Palace Green, many with placards with slogans such as “the UK is not innocent” and “silence is violence”.

Counter-protesters assembled in market square, claiming that they were to “protect” Durham market square from vandalism.  

However, according to eye-witnesses, the counter-protesters attempted to disrupt the protests multiple times at both Milburngate and Claypath.

In any event, the BLM protest was entirely peaceful and by all accounts a successful show of solidarity with the wider BLM movement.

Jerome Yates, one of the organisers of the event, stated “I was over the moon with the turnout, which was amazing give the event was only officially created on Thursday.”

He added that “we wanted to show solidarity with the protests taking place across America and the UK today and to stress the need to change and action, especially with regards to education of injustices, biases and micro-aggressions that hurt People of Colour every single day.”
